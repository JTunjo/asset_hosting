Protocol 01

Natural · 80–96 Hours · Simple & Controllable

Reach 80–96h safely Avoid mold & acetic spikes Preserve fruit sweetness No starter needed

Why It Works

  • Whole cherry = natural buffer
  • Semi-anaerobic tank controls O₂
  • Water layer stabilizes temp
  • Daily mixing prevents hot spots

General Considerations

  • Only fully ripe cherries (float test)
  • Target temp: 24–30 °C
  • Avoid sealed anaerobic w/o valve
  • Food-grade plastic tank with lid
  • No direct sun exposure

Ingredients (100 kg cherry)

  • 100 kg ripe cherry
  • 60–70 L clean water (non-chlorinated)
  • 1 kg molasses (only if °Brix <18)
  • No inoculum required
Fermentation Steps
01

Sorting — Day 0

  • Float cherries in water
  • Remove floaters and defects
  • Drain for 30 minutes
02

Tank Loading

  • Add cherries to tank
  • Add water until cherries are 70–80% submerged
  • Do NOT fully flood — reduces acetic risk
  • Target headspace: 20–30%
  • Cover with lid (not airtight)
03

0–24 Hours

Temp26–32 °C
pH~5.5 → 4.8–5.0
Stir1× at 12h

Fermentation begins spontaneously — yeast-first. Stir once at 12h mark.

04

24–48 Hours

CO₂Active
Stir2× daily

Light fruit alcohol aroma. Monitor temperature — avoid >33 °C.

⚠ If temp exceeds 33 °C: Move tank to shade + add 5–10 L cool water
05

48–80 Hours

pH4.0–4.2
Stir1× daily

LAB begins dominating. Fruit esters intensify.

✔ Sweet fermented fruit aroma = on track
✘ Vinegar smell = too much oxygen — seal more tightly
06

80–96 Hours — Finish Window

Stop fermentation when all conditions are met:

pH3.9–4.1
MucilageSlippery feel
SmellNo acetic

Drain immediately once conditions are confirmed.

Drying — Natural

📅 Day 1–2

  • Spread thin — 2–3 cm layer
  • Shade drying first 24h (critical)
  • Turn every hour

☀️ Day 3–7+

  • Gradually increase sun exposure
  • Never exceed 40 °C surface temp
  • Target 11–11.5% moisture

📊 Timeline

Drying time in Batangas humidity: 10–16 days

Protocol 02

Depulped · LAB → Wild Yeast Sequential

Reduce astringency Promote lactic smoothness Fruit complexity 72–96h total

General Considerations

  • Depulp same day
  • Avoid excessive washing (keep mucilage)
  • Strict temp control first 24–36h
  • Total target: 72–96h

Ingredients (100 kg cherry)

  • ~55–60 kg depulped parchment
  • 20 L clean water
  • 1–2 L LAB culture (backslopped or yogurt whey 1:5)
  • 500 g molasses (optional)
  • No commercial yeast needed
Fermentation Steps
⬡ Phase 1 — Lactic Stage · 0–36h
01

Depulp

Remove skin — keep mucilage intact. Do not over-wash.

02

Tank Setup

  • Add parchment to tank
  • Add 20 L water
  • Add LAB inoculum (2–3%)
  • Mix thoroughly
  • Target: semi-anaerobic — close lid loosely
03

0–24h · Critical Window

Temp28–32 °C
pH5.5 → 4.5 in ~18–24h
StirNone first 18h

LAB dominates early. Aroma: yogurt, mild sweetness. This stage degrades mucilage pectins, softens harsh phenolics, and reduces astringency.

⚠ Do NOT stir during first 18h
04

24–36h

pH target4.2–4.4

Stir once. At 36h: open lid fully — introduce oxygen to transition to Phase 2.

⬡ Phase 2 — Wild Yeast Expression · 36–84h
05

36–60h

Temp26–30 °C
pH→ 3.8–4.0
Stir2× daily

Oxygen allows native yeast to develop fruit esters. Aroma becomes tropical / wine-like.

06

60–84h — Finish Window

Stop when all conditions are met:

AromaClean fruit
SmellNo acetic
MucilagePartially broken

Total fermentation: 72–96h max. Drain and lightly rinse (optional).

✔ Maximum total fermentation: 72–96h
Drying — Depulped / Honey Style

📅 Day 1

  • Thick layer — 3–4 cm
  • Shade first 24h
  • Turn every 1–2 hours

☀️ Day 2–5

  • Gradual sun exposure
  • Avoid case hardening
  • If sticky → increase airflow (not sun)

📊 Target

Moisture: 10.8–11.2%

Timeline: 8–12 days
